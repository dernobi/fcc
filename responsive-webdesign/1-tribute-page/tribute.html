Otto Hahn

Otto Hahn, 1965

Hahn was a German chemist, pioneer in the fields of radioactivity and radiochemistry and best known for discovery and proof of nuclear fission, which is used for nuclear reactors and is the basis for nuclear weapons.

The following is a very brief summary of his life:


1879-1903: Born in Frankfurt am Main; took early interest in chemistry; took his Abitur; began to Study in Marburg; recieved his doctorate
1904–1906: Research in London and Montreal; discovered new radioactive elements;
1906–1944: Research in Berlin; Discovery of mesothorium I (radium-228); Discovery of radioactive recoil; Marriage with Edith Junghans; World War I; Discovery of protactinium; Discovery of nuclear isomerism; Discovery of nuclear fission; won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry
1945: Internment in England
1968: Died on July 28th in Göttingen
